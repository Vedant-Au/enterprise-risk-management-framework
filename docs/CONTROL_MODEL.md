# Control and escalation model

## Three layers

1. **Oversight** — the board approves appetite, receives exceptions and challenges treatment decisions.
2. **Ownership** — business-unit leaders own risks, controls and actions.
3. **Intelligence** — a common taxonomy, register and KRI rhythm aggregate evidence across the group.

## Exposure scoring

`inherent risk = likelihood (1-5) × impact (1-5)`

- Green: 1-6 — accept or monitor
- Amber: 7-14 — review and manage
- Red: 15-25 — escalate immediately

Residual risk is a fresh post-control assessment. It is not mechanically reduced by an assumed control percentage; evidence and accountable judgement are required.

## Choosing the right technique

`FMEA RPN = severity × occurrence × detectability`

Use FMEA for process failure modes. Use bow-tie analysis when causes, a central event, preventive controls, mitigations and consequences must be visible. Introduce scenario and simulation methods only after ownership and historical data are reliable.
